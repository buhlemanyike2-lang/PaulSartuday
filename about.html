History of the Institute

Paul Morutse Institute Saturday School was founded with a vision to bridge educational gaps and provide quality supplementary education to learners in our community. Named after the legendary educator Paul Morutse, our institute carries forward his legacy of excellence, dedication, and transformative teaching.

Since our inception, we have been committed to nurturing young minds, fostering academic excellence, and empowering learners to reach their full potential. Our institute has grown from a small community initiative to a recognized center of academic support, touching hundreds of lives and creating pathways to success.

Saturday School Programme

The Saturday School Programme was established in April 2024 with the aim of providing additional academic support to learners who require assistance in key school subjects.

April 2024 - Foundation

The programme initially began with Grade 11 Mathematical Literacy Classes consisting of approximately 10 learners.

First Year Success

In its first year, the programme produced outstanding results. Learners advanced to Grade 12 with notable improvements - two earned top three rankings in Mathematical Literacy, and one secured a distinction in the subject, reflecting the effectiveness of the support provided.

2025 - Expansion

Following this success, the programme expanded in 2025 to include additional subjects and support more learners. The subjects introduced included Mathematics and Physical Sciences (Grade 11), History and Business Studies.

Continued Excellence

The programme continued to produce positive academic outcomes. In History (Grade 12), one learner achieved top 3% position in their school. In 2025, the programme expanded to support senior phase learners (Grade 8 and 9), particularly in Mathematics, where strong progress has been observed.

2026 - New Addition

This year, English has been introduced to strengthen language proficiency and academic performance.
